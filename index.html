Backed by science

This isn't just a feeling. The research is clear.

The Nutrition Source

Mindful eating leads to greater psychological wellbeing, increased pleasure when eating, and body satisfaction. It addresses shame and guilt by promoting a non-judgmental attitude, and builds the skill of distinguishing emotional hunger from physical hunger.

PubMed · 68 studies reviewed

A structured review of 68 published studies found mindfulness-based approaches are most effective at addressing binge eating, emotional eating, and eating in response to external cues.

Springer

Mindful eating has been associated with reduced anxiety, depression, and loss of confidence, suggesting that cultivating awareness around eating contributes to broader mental health outcomes.

Nature

Greater amounts of mindfulness practice lead to increased behavioral flexibility, which can help break compulsive eating patterns.
